Smart Vision Works Design To Delivered UVU SLSS

Utah Valley University

Student Leadership and Success Studies

Introduction

Utah Valley University’s Student Leadership and Success Studies. Quite a mouthful of name for a department. The department focuses on classes around soft skills such as leadership, stress management, and major/career exploration. As a team with 4 other Web Design and Development students were assigned the task of helping redesign their website. We competed against other teams of classmates for which redesign the client (the head of the department) would like best.

slide 2
slide 3

Department lacked recognition, website has not main CTA

We found from student surveys and interviews and client interviews that the main problems facing the UVU SLSS was that they lacked a lot of recognition for their classes. Students had often heard of some of the classes they offered but didn’t understand the connection to the wider department.

Our first idea was that the website needed to capitalize on these classes that students already knew about. Our assumption was that the current site didn’t explain what SLSS was or what they had to offer. We wanted to study what people understood from their current website before we jumped into the designs to see if our assumptions were right.

We performed eye tracking tests and user interviews which proved most of our assumptions were correct. Users had a hard time finding the classes they offered and how to sign up for them. Users didn’t understand the mission statement of the department and had a hard time finding what the department was really about.

Wireframes sitemap and Design Critique

After our research was completed we got into some designing we went through the typical site map and whiteboard wireframes and then ideating on some of the ideas. We broke out some of the pages into similar ideas and assigned them to group members. The main page I was assigned was the landing page.

We received feedback in class from our professor and our peers about our designs and then made some initial surface comps and then again did another design review and got some more feedback which got us to our final design ready to present.

slide 2
slide 3

The Results - a new landing page with a clear CTA and better hierarchy

The landing page was my main focus as its the initial impression of the visitors of the site and would be the draw to have them explore more of the site. Our landing page was first focused on the main call to action which we decided should be meeting with a advisor because from our research we found that besides hearing from family and friends, advisors were the biggest reason students took SLSS classes. I decided to use UVU’s card components pretty heavily in my design to show some of SLSS offerings. This gave the site some actionable items and helped students better understand what SLSS was all about.

Presentation

We presented our designs to the clients along with the other teams here is what the clients had to say about our designs.

“Probably my favorite presentation. I really like how you used the data, the design of the site, the rationale, etc. Knocked it out of the park!”

Overall this was a great project working with a real client, having to work with a team, and competing against other teams. In the end we were selected with another team to hand off our designs to the development class which I wasn’t a part of but as far as I know is currently in development.
