TagAlong

UVU Design 2 Delivered Competition

Design competition for a community building event app.

UVU’s product design club hosted a design competition called “Design 2 Delivered.” The goal of the competition being to have a local business get design guidance from a team of students. With a team of 4 other we won the competition judged by a panel of 2 business owners and our professors.

The local business we designed for, TagAlong, was several months away from their launch of their app. They gave 3 goals 15,000 users, increase user engagement and retention, and enhancing the UX experience with a budget of 10,000 dollars.

TagAlong is an app to meet new people doing events such as hiking, ice climbing, movie nights, etc. Very similar to Bumble Friends or Facebook groups but with some additional features, such as carpool organizer, flake score (sort out flakey people), and advanced sort of who comes to events.

Finding pain points and early assumptions

Our first meeting as a team we all had the opportunity to use to their beta app and make a list of problems we saw, bugs we noticed, and create some early assumptions of ways we would solve them. We used these early assumptions as ways to guide what to research and what questions we needed to ask.

I came up with our team name “team get in get out”. We wanted the users to be able to get into the app quickly and get to an event and get out there. The real value and what we believe will keep new users is having value they can’t get from anywhere else. If the users have a hard time getting to an event they won’t use the app. So that being the main focus of our changes we were ready to problem solve.

Testing our assumptions to find our pain points

Based on our initial assumptions we thought the onboarding took too long so we tested similar apps we found the app averaged over 2:00 minutes than similar apps. We redesigned a simpler version that was more visually interesting.

Another assumption we had was their text message (their main way to share events with people without the app) lacked visual interest. We tested this with making our own version with shorter, better copy and a image from the event. We conducted interviews and eye tracking I think the previous design could be fit into this one quote from one of the interviews. “When I saw the long link and text I thought it was a scam” and the new design could be summarized with this quote “I would go to that event”

We found a design issue with one of their main call to actions. On the page to join or decline going to an event the buttons were so circular people didn’t know they were buttons. We tested this with user interview and usability tests. After we were sure it was the button shape we made a change in the design of the buttons and then did an eye tracking test to make sure.

Our pitch to the business owners

UI changes wasn’t the only thing we suggested to do. Our business/marketing plan was mostly inspired from a quote by Paul Graham from Y-Combinator “The most common unscalable thing founders have to do at the start is to recruit users manually. Nearly all startups have to. You can't wait for users to come to you. You have to go out and get them.”

We decided that to have a successful launch and to help their users “Get in Get Out” They needed to have professional events for the users to get out to. We came up with some event guidelines and budget allocation for 5 large events so the app wouldn’t be empty upon it’s inital launch.

Our group and presented our idea to a group of digital media professors and the 2 business owners. We gave them our budget allocation and gave them the changes to the UI to focus the ease of a user getting in and getting out and they events the user would go to. Turns out it was a pretty great solution and we ended up winning the competition.
